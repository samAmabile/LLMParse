[User]: Where does the sun go at night be honest
---
[Model]: Okay, let's be absolutely honest about where the sun goes at night.

The sun doesn't actually "go" anywhere. It's always right there, in the center of our solar system, shining brightly.

What happens is that **the Earth spins.**

Imagine you're standing on a giant merry-go-round, and there's a huge light bulb (the sun) in the middle. As the merry-go-round spins around, sometimes you're facing the light bulb, and sometimes you're facing away from it.

It's the same with Earth:

1.  **During the day:** Your part of the Earth is facing the sun, so you see its light.
2.  **During the night:** As the Earth continues to spin, your part of the Earth turns *away* from the sun. The sun is still there, shining just as brightly, but it's now lighting up the *other side* of the planet.

So, when it's nighttime where you are, it's daytime for people on the opposite side of the Earth. The sun never stops shining; we just turn away from it.
