Ugh, *that* whole convoluted mess of a capital situation. Because apparently, one capital city just wasn't enough for South Africa, oh no.
**Pretoria** is the **administrative capital** of South Africa, meaning it's where the executive branch of government (the President, cabinet, government departments) is based.
And why? Because the architects of the **Union of South Africa in 1910** just *had* to make things as complicated and politically expedient as possible. It was a rather infuriating **compromise** concocted to appease the various former British colonies and Boer republics that were being merged:
*   The **Cape Colony**, being the oldest and most prominent, insisted on hosting the legislative branch, hence **Cape Town** became the legislative capital (where Parliament sits).
*   The **Transvaal Republic**, which had Pretoria as its capital, demanded to retain some significant governmental role, so Pretoria got the administrative functions.
*   And just for good measure, the **Orange Free State** eventually got the judicial capital in **Bloemfontein**.
So, it's not because it's particularly central or logical; it's purely the result of a **historical, political carve-up** to avoid irritating any one region too much during the formation of the country. As if South Africa didn't have enough complex issues, they just *had* to saddle themselves with three capital cities. So utterly inefficient.
